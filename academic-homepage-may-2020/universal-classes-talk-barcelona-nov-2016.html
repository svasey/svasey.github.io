When does categoricity imply amalgamation?

This is the title of a talk to be given at the 2016 Young researchers' seminar week, at the Centre de Recerca Matemàtica (Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona) on November 7, 2016.

Abstract

Abstract elementary classes (AECs) are an axiomatic framework encompassing classes of models of an L∞, ω theory, as well as numerous algebraic examples. They were introduced by Saharon Shelah forty years ago. Shelah focused on generalizations of Morley's categoricity theorem and conjectured the following eventual version: An AEC categorical in a high-enough cardinal is categorical on a tail of cardinals. The conjecture has been used as a measuring stick to judge progress in the development of a stability theory for AECs. Despite numerous approximations, the conjecture is still open.

In this talk, I will discuss a related (perhaps more set-theoretic) conjecture, due to Grossberg: Categoricity in a high-enough cardinal should imply eventual amalgamation. I will survey known approximations, including my recent result that both Grossberg and Shelah's conjectures hold in classes of models of a universal L∞, ω theory.
